History Of BCI

  • 1780
    Electromyostimulation
    Luigi Galvani discoveres that the muscles of a dead frog could be stimulated through an electrical spark.
  • 1800
    Auditory Stimulation
    Alessandro Volta managed to stimulate his internal ear by using electrical current.
  • 1870
    Neural Stimulation
    Edward Hitzig and Gustav Fritsch discover stimulating a dog's brain using electricity allowed them to cause muscle movements.
  • 1874
    Human Neural Stimulation
    Roberts Bartholow experiments with electricity in the brain of a woman with a hole in her head.
  • 1924
    Electroencephalogram
    Hanns Berger is the first person to record human brain activity with an EEG.
  • 1949
    Stimoceiver
    Jose Manuel Rodriguez Delgado, a ophthalmologist invented an implanted electrode system inside animals that established two-way communications with the brain. This allowed Jose to stimulate different regions, producing changes in affect and behavior of the animal. He provided this technology to patients with chronic refractory epilepsy and schizophrenia.
  • 1950
    Animal Electrical Stimulation
    Delgado published 134 scientific publications about electrical stimulations he had made in cats, monkeys and patients, both psychotic and non-psychotic.
  • 1952
    Stopping a Bull
    Jose Delgado stops a charging bull by controlling its behavior using electrodes.
  • 1952
    Chlorpromazine
    Bob Heath, a scientist at the Tulane University, invented chlorpromazine which he used on patients with schizophrenia. This is considered the beginning of the neuropsychopharmacology revolution.
  • 1957
    Cochlear Implants
    André Djourno, a professor of medical physics, and Charles Eyriès, Parisian otologist, tried to restore the facial nerve function of a patient who had extensive bilateral cholesteatoma.
  • 1961
    Auditory Percepts
    William House, an otologist, and John Doyle, a neurosurgeon, developed an electrode that they placed through the round window in two patients which changed loudness and pitch with the variation in the rate of stimulation.
  • 1962
    Neural Prosthetics
    Liberson invented a peroneal nerve stimulator to treat foot drop after hemiplegia.
  • 1972
    Psychocivilized Society
    Delgado wrote a book encouraging the development of a future psychocivilized human being, which suggested controlling people's morality to create a less cruel, happier, and better man.
  • 1984
    Multichannel Cochlear Implant
    Graeme Clark an ENT professor, conducted basic research on the pathophysiology of profound deafness in animals and on the tolerability of implanted materials, which resulted in the creation of the First Multichannel Cochlear Implant System.
  • 1996
    Combating Paralasis
    Neurotrophic Electrodes are implanted in paralysed man allowing him to control a computer cursor.
  • 2004
    Braingate
    A paralyzed man was able to control a computer cursor using a brain chip developed by Cyberkinetics.
  • 2005
    Prosthetics
    A man with tetraplegia is able to control a robotic arm using a brain computer interface created as part of the BrainGate Project.
  • 2016
    NeuraLink
    Elon Musk launches Neuralink to develop ultra high bandwidth brain machine interfaces.
  • 2024
    First Brain Chip
    Noland Arbaugh is the first person to receive a brain chip, developed by Neuralink.
